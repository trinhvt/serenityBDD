class="container"><article id="why" class="text-center cucumber-bdd-cucumber"><h1>Cucumber makes your team <strong>amazing</strong></h1><p class="lead">At a glance, Cucumber might just look like another tool for running automated tests.<br /><strong>But it’s more than that.</strong></p></article><div class="row"><div class="cucumber-bdd-benefit col-md-5"><h2>A single source of truth</h2><p>Cucumber merges specification and test documentation into one cohesive whole.</p></div><div class="cucumber-bdd-benefit col-md-5 col-md-offset-2"><h2>Living documentation</h2><p>Because they're automatically tested by Cucumber, your specifications
are always bang up-to-date.</p></div><div class="cucumber-bdd-benefit col-md-5"><h2>Focus on the customer</h2><p>Business and IT don't always understand each other. Cucumber's
<em> executable specifications</em> encourage
closer collaboration, helping teams keep the business goal in
mind at all times.</p></div><div class="cucumber-bdd-benefit col-md-5 col-md-offset-2"><h2>Less rework</h2><p>When automated testing is this much fun, teams can easily protect themselves from
costly regressions.</p></div></div><div class="row"><div class="cucumber-bdd-benefit col-md-8 col-md-offset-2"><img src="/images/home/cucumber-awesome.jpg" class="img-responsive" alt="Cucumber is awesome" /></div></div><hr class="simple-separator" /><div class="row"><div class="col-md-6"><article id="how" class="cucumber-bdd-bdd"><h1 class="text-right"><small>Cucumber is for</small> Behaviour-Driven Development</h1><figure><img src="/images/home/bdd-cycle.png" class="img-responsive" alt="BDD cycle illustration" /><figcaption>Illustration by <a href="http://thepaulrayner.com/about/">Paul Rayner</a> (Thanks!) </figcaption></figure></article></div><div class="col-md-offset-1 col-md-5"><div class="cucumber-bdd-bdd"><p>First, the developers, testers and business folks explore the problem
domain, and collaborate to produce concrete examples that describe
the behaviour they want.</p><p>Sometimes that discussion is hard, because it throws up all the
misunderstandings and assumptions you’d normally discover
much later on.</p><p>But that's a good thing.</p><p>Next, the team use Cucumber to run those examples as automated
acceptance tests.</p><p>As the team work on the solution, Cucumber tells you which examples
are implemented and working, and warns you about the ones that aren’t.</p><p>Before you know it, you have one document that’s both the specification
and the tests for your software.</p></div></div></div></div></section><section id="community" class="community"><div class="container"><div class="row"><div class="community-download text-center
